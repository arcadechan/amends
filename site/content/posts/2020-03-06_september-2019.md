Welcome to the first post of the blog which happens to also be a _Monthly Summary_ post. In _Monthly Summaries_, simply labelled by the month, I'll recap all the best finds for that month. In order to catch up to the current month, I'll be publishing one monthly summary per week.

September showed a fair share of folksy artists but also a decent portion of indie and some electronic.

---

<iframe src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/track/49DmTlbizOa1VTQRqVAuWJ" width="300" height="380" frameborder="0" allowtransparency="true" allow="encrypted-media"></iframe>

This is such a fun song with so many dreamy hooks and changes in it. The subtle echoing synth in the background paints pictures of water seascapes, and the general reverb of everything makes one feel completely submerged in some kind of super soft and silky sound blanket.

The Dears really are something else that I can't get enough of. Lot's of great bass playing in the first two tracks. They feel orchestral pop/rock with vocals like Morrissey's.

_No Return_ has a cool shift in style at 4:08 with a fantastic fill at 4:18.
_Where The World Begins And Ends_ picks up tempo in a real wonderful way at 3:20.
_22: The Death of All the Romance_ has lovely back and forth between the male and female vocalists, that adds to the theme.
_Lost in the Plot's_ drop in volume at 2:06 really helps augment the bridge at 3:21 and the kick in to high gear at 3:36.

<iframe src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/track/77qRetUgTCYUCQ2nSeHIhx" width="300" height="380" frameborder="0" allowtransparency="true" allow="encrypted-media"></iframe>

Ah yes. Back to folksy! The raw emotion in _Lady of the Ark_ becomes really palpable in his rise in pitch at 1:06 and throughout the rest of the song. There are subtle tones throughout the song that fill it just right. The super reverb-ridden tambourine, the organ at 1:21, the backing vocals at 1:37.

Oh! There's also a sick accordion at 2:34.

The 60s at their best. _Painting Box_ has really lovely guitar playing with equally great vocals. _Chinese White_ is a lot more of what people imagine when they hear _the 60s_, but the way in which the vocals sustain their key at 1:19 while the instruments drop and rise in theirs, is what really sold this track for me. That violin sounding thing is really cool too! It's called a gimbri, which is a type of lute.

Ariel Pink makes amazing songs, dips them in some kind of lo-fi aging chemical, and makes them even more amazing. The hooks this man is able to come up with are mind boggling.

MIND BOGGLING HOOKS:
- Lipstick
    - _"Running for cover. Oh oh oh"_ at 0:46
    - The entire chorus from 0:56 to 1:10
    - The outro at 2:50
- White Freckles
    - The entire intro from 0:00 to 0:41 (including the "HEY!" at 0:34)
    - The shift at 0:42
- Revolution's A Lie
    - The entire song because Joy Divison
- Butt-House Blondies
    - The crisp arpeggios and calmness of the verses
- Round And Round
    - The _"Na na na na's"_ everywhere
    - The really quiet _"Breakdown, break break breakdown"_ at 1:01
    - The "YOUUU-HUUU PLAY". You'll hear it!
    - The chorus. It's just out of this world. At 1:58
